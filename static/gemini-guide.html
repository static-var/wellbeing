Gemini guide

Bring your own Gemini key, with eyes open.

This page shows you how to get a Gemini API key, what the model presets roughly feel like, and the privacy line you should understand first: if you choose to use your own Gemini key, your requests go to Google and Google's own terms, privacy practices, quotas, and retention rules apply.

Best default gemini-2.5-flash is usually the safest everyday pick for supportive chat: fast, capable, and easier on quota than a heavier model.
Highest depth gemini-2.5-pro should feel more thoughtful and nuanced, but it will generally cost more quota and can be slower.
Budget option gemini-2.0-flash is a lighter, lower-friction option when you mostly want quick check-ins and less quota pressure.
Important privacy note When you enable a personal Gemini key in Wellbeing, we forward your chat request to Google's Gemini API using the key you supplied. Google may log, retain, process, or inspect metadata and request content under its own policies. We do not control Google's storage, review, training, retention, or abuse-monitoring behavior, and we are not responsible for what Google does with the data you send through its service.

How to get a Gemini API key

Open Google AI Studio

Go to AI Studio and sign into the Google account you want tied to the key.

Create or choose a project/key

Use the API key screen to create a key, then copy it somewhere safe. In most cases it begins with something like AIza....

Paste it into Wellbeing

In onboarding or settings, enable “Use my own Gemini API key”, choose a preset model, paste the key, and save.

Review Google's terms yourself

Before you rely on a personal key, read Google's current Gemini terms and privacy pages directly. They can change over time.
